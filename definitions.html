Term Definition
ADRAlternative Dispute Resolution means the different ways people can resolve disputes without a trial.
Admiralty LawA branch of law that deals with legal matters on oceans and navigable waters.
Authorized SharesThe total number of shares a company can issue according to its corporate charter.
AcquisitionThe process of one company buying and taking control of another company.
Advisory BoardA group of experts who provide advice and guidance to a companys management.
Alter EgoA secondary or alternative identity often used to describe a legal concept where a person and their business are viewed as the same entity.
Annual MeetingA yearly gathering of a company's shareholders to discuss its performance and elect directors.
ArbitrationA process where a dispute is settled by an impartial third party outside of court.
Articles of IncorporationThe document filed to legally form a corporation.
Articles of AssociationA document that outlines a company's structure and defines its purpose and regulations.
AgentA person authorized to act on behalf of another in business or legal matters.
Agent for Service of ProcessA designated person who receives legal documents on behalf of a business.
AgreementA mutual understanding between parties about their rights and responsibilities.
BondA financial instrument where an investor loans money to an entity in exchange for periodic interest and repayment at maturity.
BylawsThe internal rules that govern a corporations operations and management.
Debenturea type of debt instrument issued by companies to raise capital.
DividendA payment made by a company to its shareholders, usually from profits.
DebtMoney borrowed that must be paid back, usually with interest.
Derivative ActionA lawsuit brought by a shareholder on behalf of the company, often against its executives or directors.
DirectorA member of a company's board who helps guide and make decisions for the company.
ConditionA requirement that must be met for a contract or legal obligation to be fulfilled.
ConsiderationSomething of value exchanged between parties in a contract.
Contract TerminationThe ending of a contract before it has been fully performed.
PartnershipA business arrangement where two or more individuals share ownership and management responsibilities.
